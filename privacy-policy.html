1. Scope & Consent

If you are under the age required by law in your country to form a binding contract, please do not use our Services without parental consent.

Note: If your app uses ads or advertising identifiers, ensure the Play Console Data Safety section marks "Yes" for collection and sharing for ads. Update the app's Data Safety form whenever you add/remove SDKs or change data practices.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

We collect PII only when you voluntarily provide it (for example, when you register, contact support or make an in-app purchase). PII may include:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Payment details (processed by third-party payment processors)

3.2 Non‑Personally Identifiable Information (Non‑PII)

Automatically collected data may include:

  • Device identifiers (Advertising ID, Device ID)
  • App usage and crash reports
  • IP address and approximate location
  • Operating system and device model

4. Permissions & Why We Request Them

We request only the permissions required for app functionality. Below are common permissions and their justification — update as per your app's actual usage before publishing.

  • INTERNET: Required to access web services, APIs, and third‑party services.
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE: To detect connectivity and handle offline scenarios.
  • LOCATION (approximate): Optional. Used only when the user enables location-based features. We do not collect fine location unless explicitly stated.
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE / WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE: If your app accesses media or files. From Android 11+, use scoped storage APIs.
  • CAMERA: Only if your app provides photo/video capture features; explicit runtime consent is required.

Important: For every permission marked "dangerous" by Android, request it at runtime with a clear explanation of why it is needed. The Play Store review may reject apps that request unrelated or excessive permissions.

5. Third‑Party SDKs, Ads & Analytics

We may use third‑party SDKs and services to provide analytics, crash reporting, advertising, and other features. These third parties may collect and process data under their own policies. Common examples include Google Firebase, Google AdMob, and other analytics or ad networks.

Action required for publishers: Ensure every SDK used in the app is declared in the Play Console Data Safety form and the SDK's data practices are accurately reflected. If you use AdMob or other ad networks, disclose that advertising data is collected and shared for ad personalization.
